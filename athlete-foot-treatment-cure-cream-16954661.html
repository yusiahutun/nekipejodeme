Athlete's foot treatment: How to cure and prevent the fungal infection

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, June 30, 2024

Athlete's foot, known medically as tinea pedis, is a common fungal infection that normally occurs between the toes.

The affected skin may be itchy, red, scaly, dry, cracked or blistered and symptoms can become worse in the summer due to humidity.

The condition is not serious but it can be unsightly and embarrassing. It’s also important to treat athlete's foot to prevent it spreading to other parts of the body or other people.

Most people pick up the infection by walking around barefoot in communal areas like gym showers or locker rooms.

What is the best way to get rid of athlete's foot?

It can normally be cured with over-the-counter antifungal treatments which are available in pharmacies like Boots and Superdrug.

Treatment comes in creams, sprays, liquids and powders and will stop fungus from growing.

You should wash and dry your feet first, then apply treatment to the affected skin and surrounding area.

Continue until the infection has cleared up entirely. If you’re not sure which antifungal medicine to use, just ask your pharmacist.

What should I do if it doesn’t clear up?

If you’ve tried over-the-counter treatments, but seen no improvement after a week you should see your GP.

They’re likely to take a small sample of skin for testing and will probably prescribe some stronger antifungal medicines.

What are the key symptoms of athlete's foot?

Athlete's foot most commonly affects the skin between the toes or on the bottom of the feet.

Affected areas of skin may be:

1. Dry, red, scaly and flaky2. White, soggy and cracked2. Itchy3. Sore4. Covered in small blisters

Athlete's foot may also spread and cause fungal nail infections.

How can I prevent athlete's foot?

There are several ways you can reduce your risk of contracting athlete's foot. These include the following:

1. Drying your feet gently but thoroughly after washing them, particularly the areas between your toes

2. Wearing cotton socks and roomy shoes made of natural materials such as leather – this can allow your feet to "breathe"

3. Wear a fresh pair of socks, tights or stockings every day

4. Avoid walking around barefoot in public areas

5. Use talcum powder on your feet to stop them getting sweaty
