How distraught can one be to tirelessly search for their kin for years? When did Brittanee Drexel disappear?

Brittanee, just like other teenagers, was curious and wanted to explore. In April 2009, she asked her mother whether she could go to Myrtle Beach, South Carolina, over spring break with her friends. What happened to Brittanee Drexel

It took 13 years for something substantial to come up about Brittanee Drexel's update. Raymond Moody, a registered sex offender who spent 21 days in prison after a 1983 abduction case in California involving and 9-year-old girl, confessed to the crime of killing Brittanee.

Interestingly, Moody had been pulled over for a traffic violation near Myrtle Beach a day after Brittanee Drexel disappeared. When he was pulled over, Raymond had scratches on his face. However, the police did not understand the connection until a decade later, when he confessed to the crime of murdering Brittanee.

In early May 2022, Raymond Moody turned himself in to Georgetown Sherriff’s Office based on obstruction of justice. This was 13 years after Brittanee Drexel’s disappearance. Moody voluntarily spoke with investigators and gave complete details of what happened on that fateful day.

Raymond stated that he and Angel spotted Brittanee leaving the hotel while driving and pulled over. They then asked her if she wanted to smoke with them (many have refuted this event), to which she complied, and they all rode back to the couple’s campsite by the Santee River pole yards north of McClellanville in Georgetown County.

After Angel left, Moody sexually assaulted Brittanee, killed and buried her. After the confessions, Raymond led investigators to the wooded area where he had buried Brittanee. Raymond’s partner, Angel, was never charged with the teenager’s murder.

On October 19, 2022, Raymond pleaded guilty to murder, kidnapping and criminal sexual conduct and was sentenced to life in prison plus two consecutive terms of 30 years.

Where was Brittanee Drexel found?

Brittanee Drexel’s remains were discovered in Georgetown County, South Carolina. The investigators were led by Raymond’s account of that fateful evening. The police believed that Raymond had buried Brittanee a day after she went missing.

Brittanee Drexel's autopsy

Forensic specialists examined Brittanee’s remains to find out more about the circumstances of her death. According to the findings, the teenager’s cause of death was ruled as “undetermined”. The conditions of her remains, considering it was more than ten years later, posed significant challenges in establishing how she died.
