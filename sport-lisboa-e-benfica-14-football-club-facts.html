The Origins of Sport Lisboa E Benfica

Sport Lisboa E Benfica, commonly known as Benfica, is a highly successful football club from Portugal. It was founded on February 28, 1904, by a group of enthusiastic football players, students, and university professors.

The Club’s Stadium: Estádio da Luz

Benfica’s home stadium is Estádio da Luz, located in Lisbon, Portugal. With a seating capacity of over 64,000 spectators, it is one of the largest stadiums in Europe. The stadium has witnessed countless remarkable moments in the club’s history.

The Eagle Mascot

One of the most iconic features of Benfica is their eagle mascot named “Vitória.” This majestic bird of prey is often seen soaring across the stadium before each home game, symbolizing the club’s strength and dominance.

European Success: Two Champions League Titles

Benfica has achieved remarkable success in European competitions. They won the prestigious UEFA Champions League twice, in the years 1961 and 1962, becoming the first club to win back-to-back titles in the modern era of the competition.

The Legendary Eusébio

Eusébio da Silva Ferreira, simply known as Eusébio, is considered one of the greatest football players in Benfica’s history. The Portuguese forward played for the club from 1960 to 1975, scoring a remarkable 638 goals in 614 official matches.

Rivalry with Sporting CP and FC Porto

Benfica has intense rivalries with Sporting CP and FC Porto, collectively known as the “Big Three” of Portuguese football. These matches, known as the “Big Three Derbies,” are highly anticipated and attract passionate fans from all over the country.

The Bela Guttman Curse

One of the most enduring myths in Benfica’s history is the “Bela Guttman Curse.” It is believed that Hungarian coach Béla Guttman, who won two European Cups with Benfica in 1961 and 1962, cursed the club after a dispute over a salary increase. Since then, Benfica has not won another European title.

The Domination of the Primeira Liga

Benfica is the most successful club in the history of the Primeira Liga, Portugal’s top division football league. They have won the league title a record 37 times, showcasing their dominance and consistency in national competitions.

The Famous Red Kit

Benfica is known for their iconic red home kit, with white sleeves and black shorts. The color red symbolizes the club’s passion, determination, and the “blood” of their supporters, creating a visually striking and recognizable identity.

A Worldwide Fanbase

Benfica has a dedicated and passionate fanbase that extends far beyond Portugal. Their supporters, known as “Benfiquistas,” can be found in all corners of the globe, proudly sporting the club’s colors and chanting their support during matches.

The Benfica Academy: A Hotbed of Talent

The Benfica Academy, also known as Caixa Futebol Campus, is renowned for its development of young talent. Many talented footballers, including Cristiano Ronaldo and Renato Sanches, have emerged from the academy, showcasing the club’s commitment to nurturing future stars.

The Record Goalscorer: Fernando Peyroteo

Fernando Peyroteo holds the record for the most goals scored by a Benfica player in a single season. In the 1937-1938 season, the Portuguese striker netted a staggering 43 goals, a record that still stands to this day.

A Strong Presence in International Competitions

Benfica has a rich history of competing in international competitions. They have reached the UEFA Europa League final on several occasions, including memorable runs in 1983, 2013, and 2014, showcasing their ability to compete against some of the best teams in Europe.
