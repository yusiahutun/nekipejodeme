World Snooker Championship stars only ended drinking contest after 42 pints each

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, May 22, 2024

Snooker and alcohol have long been closely associated given their close proximity, leading to many players achieving heroic status based not only on their talent with a cue.

Indeed, some stars of the baize are hailed for their abilities to drink in near-mythical volumes, and Bill Werbeniuk is almost inarguably the most famous of the lot. 'Big Bill' boasted a frame that could dwarf most of his competitors, and his skills for supping pints became the stuff of legend over time.

But one such story illustrated the Canadian's uncanny aptitude for quaffing quarts of amber ale, which is said to have emerged during a trip to Australia in the 1970s. It was then that he and fellow pro Eddie Sinclair agreed to a friendly match while putting each other's capacity for consumption to the test.

As a proud Scot, Sinclair held no small renown when it came to knocking back pints in great quantities. However, even he discovered he had met his match in Werbeniuk, who was the last man left standing after the pair had quaffed a combined 85 pints.

However, it was Big Bill's immediate response to the triumph that makes the tale that much more memorable. Figures from inside snooker's great halls have recalled how Werbeniuk claimed he was 'away for a proper drink', having seemingly not quenched his thirst on beer alone.

"Bill Werbeniuk against an old Scottish pro called Eddie Sinclair, that was the match of the century," seven-time world champion Stephen Hendry told The Guardian in 2001 when talking about a legendary match involving a fellow Scot. "Werbeniuk won 43 to 42.

"That's pints, by the way. Went on all day, the drinking, finished when Sinclair was on his back, unconscious. At that point Werbeniuk gives it: 'I'm away to the bar for a proper drink.'"

Former world No26 Sinclair may never have drunk quite as much for the rest of his days, though he did go on to play some of the best snooker of his career after that clash. As for Werbeniuk, a 43-pinter only scratches the surface of his swigging sagas.

It's alleged the Vancouver native used to sink at least one pint per frame as regular practice, and he supposedly had six pints before every match approved as a tax deductible expense. But even those claims pale in comparison to the story suggesting he once had 76 cans during a single game, which was a notch above his usual daily average of 40 to 50 pints.
