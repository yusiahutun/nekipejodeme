Kari Lake has made a name for herself in the media and politics. As a TV weather reporter and news anchor, she has been a prominent figure in the news industry for over 27 years. Lake is known for her strong family ties, a defining aspect of her life, shaping her into the influential figure she is today. And whenever she is on a campaign trail, she is accompanied by her husband and children.

Kari Lake is an American former television news anchor. She is also the former candidate for governor of Arizona in 2022. As of 2023, she is Arizona's current United States Senate candidate for the 2024 election.

Previously, Kari worked as a TV weather reporter and news anchor. She retired in 2021 to venture into politics. Since then, her husband has accompanied her on her campaign trail, filming each moment.

Early life

The personality was born Kari Ann Lake on 23 August 1969 in Rock Island, Illinois, United States. She is the youngest daughter of Larry A. Lake (father) and Sheila Lake (mother). Furthermore, she has eight siblings.

Kari Lake’s father, Larry A. Lake, was an educator and coached football. Her mother, Sheila Lake, was a nurse. Lake's upbringing instilled in her the importance of hard work and family values.

She completed her high school education at North Scott Senior High School in Eldridge, Iowa. Later, she earned a Bachelor of Arts in journalism and communications from the University of Iowa.

Professional career

Kari started her professional career in May 1991. She began working as an intern at KWQC-TV in Davenport, Iowa, while attending the University of Iowa. After her internship, she became a production assistant at the network.

She moved on from KWQC-TV and joined WHBF-TV in Rock Island in 1992. The network hired her as a daily reporter and weekend weathercaster.

In August 1994, Lake moved to Phoenix, Arizona, United States, to work for KPNX. The network hired her as the weekend weather anchor, and she later became the evening anchor at KPNX. After four years at KPNX in Arizona, Lake moved to Albany, New York, to work for WNYT in 1998.

After one year in New York, the former news anchor returned to Arizona to work as an evening anchor for KSAZ-TV (Fox 10 Phoenix). She worked for the network for over two decades before retiring in early 2021.

In June 2021, Lake announced her candidacy for Governor of Arizona in the 2022 election as a Republican despite being a Democrat between 2008 and 2012. She won the Republican nomination in the August primary but lost the election to Katie Hobbs in November 2022.

On 10 October 2023, Kari Lake announced her candidacy for Arizona's 2024 United States Senate election.

Husband and children

The American politician is married to Jeff Halperin and has two children. Jeff is an American news anchor, journalist, videographer, and business owner. He was born on 20 September 1967. He holds a bachelor's degree in journalism and history from Creighton University.

Kari Lake's husband has been a news photographer at NBC News from 1994 to 1998, covering national and international events. He also worked as a news photographer at WNYT-TV in Albany, New York, from 1998 to 2000, where he met Kari.

After meeting at work, the couple married on 26 September 1998. On 30 March 2003, Kari and Jeff welcomed their first child, a daughter named Ruby Halperin. Five years later, they welcomed their second child, a son named Leo Halperin, on 10 October 2008.

Kari Lake's children have been seen attending their mother's political rallies and events. Her husband has also supported her political career, providing videography for her campaign, especially for the Latino community. Kari Lake's family lives in Phoenix, Arizona.

Dating history

Jeff Halperin is not Kari Lake’s first husband. The former TV news anchor was married before meeting Jeff. Her first husband is Tracy Finnegan, an electrical engineer. Their marriage started in 1991. Unfortunately, their union ended in divorce after a relatively brief period.
