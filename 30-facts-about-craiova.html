The Name Craiova

The name Craiova is believed to have originated from the Romanian word “crai” which means “prince.” This name reflects the city’s historical significance as a former royal residence during the reign of Prince Mihail the Brave in the 16th century.

Oltenia’s Cultural and Economic Hub

Craiova is often referred to as the cultural and economic heart of the historical province of Oltenia. The city plays a crucial role in Romania’s cultural heritage and has been a center for arts, education, and industry.

The Birthplace of Constantin Brancusi

Craiova is the birthplace of the world-renowned sculptor Constantin Brancusi. Brancusi is considered one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, known for his unique abstract sculptures that revolutionized modern art.

The Oldest Theater in Romania

Craiova is home to the Oltenia Philharmonic and the National Theater “Marin Sorescu,” which is the oldest theater in Romania. The theater has a rich history, dating back to 1850, and has hosted numerous renowned plays and performances.

University City

Craiova is a thriving university city, housing some of Romania’s top educational institutions. The University of Craiova, founded in 1947, is one of the most prestigious universities in the country and attracts students from all over Romania and beyond.

Magnificent Oltenia Art Museum

The Oltenia Art Museum, located in Craiova, houses an extensive collection of artworks spanning various periods, including medieval, Renaissance, and modern art. The museum is a testament to the city’s appreciation and celebration of artistic expression.

The Historic Craiova Fortress

One of Craiova’s iconic landmarks is the Craiova Fortress, a medieval fortress with a rich history dating back to the 14th century. The fortress has witnessed numerous battles and serves as a reminder of the city’s strategic importance throughout the ages.

The Craiova Musical Fountain

The Craiova Musical Fountain is a mesmerizing attraction that combines water, light, and sound in a harmonious spectacle. Located in the city center, the fountain attracts visitors who come to witness the captivating performances accompanied by music.

The Pride of Romanian Football

Craiova is synonymous with football in Romania. The city is home to FC Universitatea Craiova, one of the country’s most successful football clubs. The team has achieved numerous victories, making Craiova a hub of football enthusiasm.

The Baniei House

One of Craiova’s architectural gems is the Baniei House, a historic building dating back to the 18th century. It showcases a blend of Baroque and Neoclassical architectural styles and serves as a cultural center, hosting various exhibitions and events.

The Garden of Repose Memorial Park

The Garden of Repose is a tranquil memorial park in Craiova, dedicated to soldiers who lost their lives during World War I. The park serves as a place of remembrance and reflection, inviting visitors to pay tribute to those who made the ultimate sacrifice.

The Electric Tram Network

Craiova boasts one of the oldest electric tram networks in Romania, providing an efficient and eco-friendly mode of transportation. The trams offer a convenient way to explore the city and enjoy its landmarks and attractions.

The Traditional Oltenian Cuisine

Craiova is a paradise for food enthusiasts, particularly those eager to savor the flavors of Oltenian cuisine. Traditional dishes such as “ciorb? de peri?oare” (meatball soup) and “m?m?lig? cu brânz?” (polenta with cheese) showcase the region’s culinary delights.

The Craiova Central Park

Craiova Central Park is a sprawling green oasis in the heart of the city. It offers a peaceful escape from the urban hustle and bustle, with walking paths, recreational areas, and a serene lake where visitors can relax and unwind.

The International Theater Festival

Craiova hosts an annual International Theater Festival, attracting theater companies and artists from around the world. The festival showcases a diverse range of performances, fostering cultural exchange and celebrating the art of theater.

The Metropolitan Cathedral

The Metropolitan Cathedral, also known as “St. Demetrius,” is a stunning architectural masterpiece in Craiova. The cathedral’s elegant design and ornate decorations make it a must-visit landmark for those interested in religious and cultural heritage.

The Magnolia Square

Magnolia Square is a charming public square in Craiova adorned with beautiful magnolia trees. It is a popular gathering spot, perfect for strolling, socializing, and enjoying the vibrant atmosphere of the city.

The Nicolae Romanescu Park

Nicolae Romanescu Park is the largest natural park in Romania and is located in Craiova. With its picturesque landscapes, serene lakes, and diverse flora and fauna, the park offers a serene retreat for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

The International Ceramics Biennale

Craiova hosts the International Ceramics Biennale, a prestigious event that celebrates the art of ceramics. Artists from all over the world showcase their talent and creativity, making it a hub for ceramic enthusiasts and collectors.

The Craiova Botanical Research Center

The Craiova Botanical Research Center is a renowned institution dedicated to botanical research and preservation. It plays a vital role in studying and conserving the diverse plant species found in Romania and beyond.

The Beautiful Churches of Craiova

Craiova is home to numerous magnificent churches, each boasting unique architectural styles and religious significance. From the impressive St. Andrew’s Church to the elegant St. Dumitru Church, these religious landmarks are worth exploring.

The Craiova City Hall

The Craiova City Hall is an architectural gem that showcases the elegance of late 19th-century design. Its grandeur and intricate details make it an iconic symbol of the city’s administrative center.

The Multicultural Craiova

Craiova is a melting pot of cultures, with diverse communities coexisting harmoniously. This multiculturalism is reflected in the city’s gastronomy, traditions, and artistic expressions.

The Oltenia Museum

The Oltenia Museum houses an impressive collection of archaeological artifacts, historical documents, and ethnographic displays. It provides valuable insights into the region’s past and showcases its cultural heritage.

The Craiova Zoo

The Craiova Zoo is an enjoyable destination for wildlife enthusiasts. It houses a wide variety of animal species, ranging from exotic mammals and reptiles to colorful birds, providing an educational and entertaining experience for visitors of all ages.

The Ion Oblemenco Stadium

The Ion Oblemenco Stadium is a state-of-the-art sports facility in Craiova, named after the legendary Romanian football player. It serves as the home stadium for FC Universitatea Craiova and has a seating capacity of over 30,000 spectators.
