Blake Shelton, Adam Levine, More

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, May 3, 2024

The Voice has been on for more than 20 seasons, and while none of its winners have become household names, the show always has top-tier coaches. We know about their salaries, but what about The Voice judge’s net worths? Well, like their paychecks, The Voice judges aren’t on the same levels of wealth. But that doesn’t mean they’re paid pennies either. Each of the coaches is a millionaire (obvi), but which one is worth the most? We do the investigative work ahead.

The Voice premiered in April 2011 with coaches Blake Shelton, Adam Levine, Christina Aguilera and Cee Lo Green. Immediately, the show became a hit for its simple but irresistible premise: You can only judge singers on their voice. In the audition rounds, the judges’ chairs are turned around as contestants perform. If they like a contestant and want them on their team, they press the red button in front of them, which turns around their chairs and allows them to see the singer. If more than one judge presses his or her button, the contestant is allowed to choose the celebrity they want as their coach—but not before a hilarious bidding war between the famous judges.

While the voice-only premise just lasts for the audition rounds, it still makes for a fun and innovative singing competition. It’s no wonder The Voice has been on TV for almost a decade. Find out how much each of the show’s past and present judges are worth ahead.

  • CeeLo Green

    Seasons: 1 – 3, 5

    Net worth: $10 million

    CeeLo was one of The Voice’s four OG judges when the show premiered in 2011. Though his net worth is the lowest of the 14 coaches—at $10 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth—the singer will always hold a special place in our hearts as an OG whose sunglasses slayed each episode. Cee Lo, who’s best known as a member of Gnarls Barkley, has five Grammys and one top 10 hit on the Billboard Hot 100, “Fuck You,” which reached number 2 in 2010.

  • Dan + Shay

    Dan + Shay Net Worth: $5 million

    Dan + Shay’s net worth each is $5 million. According to Fresher’s Live, Shay Mooney’s net worth is $5 million and similarly, Dan Smyer’s net worth is around $5 million according to Buzz Learn.

  • Camilla Cabello

    Season 22

    Net worth: $18 million

    Camila Cabello joined The Voice on season 22 in 2022. According to Celebrity Net Worth, she’s worth $18 million, which comes from her salary on The Voice; her two albums with Fifth Harmony: 2015’s Reflection and 2016’s 7/27; her three solo studio albums: 2018’s Camila, 2019’s Romance and 2022’s Familia; and her starring role in Amazon Prime Video’s 2021 live-action Cinderella movie.

  • Chance The Rapper

    Season: 23

    Net worth: $25 million

    One of Chance The Rapper’s significant career moments came with the release of Coloring Book in 2016, which won him multiple Grammy Awards, including Best Rap Album. Notably, the album was groundbreaking for being the first streaming-only album to receive a Grammy nomination and win. He joined The Voice for one season in 23 and has an estimated net worth of $25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.

  • Jennifer Hudson

    Seasons 13, 15 (The Voice UK Seasons 6, 7, 8)

    Net worth: $30 million

    Hudson was a judge on The Voice in seasons 13 and 15. While her net worth isn’t the highest of the coaches, it’s still a sizable number for us normal folk. According to Celebrity Net Worth, Hudson is worth $30 million. Hudson has two Grammys and is known for songs like “Spotlight” and “If This Isn’t Love.” Along with her music career, she’s also an established actress with an Oscar (!!! for 2005’s Dreamgirls) and roles in films like Cats and Sex and the City. Not bad for a former American Idol contestant.

  • Kelly Clarkson

    Seasons: 14 – 21, 23

    Net worth: $45 million

    Clarkson is one of the highest-paid judges on The Voice with a $14 million per season salary. While her net worth isn’t the highest of her peers, the former American Idol winner’s paychecks speak for themselves. Clarkson has eight top 10 albums and multiple top 10 singles, including “Since U Been Gone” and “Stronger.” According to Celebrity Net Worth, her net worth is $45 million.

  • Niall Horan

    Seasons: 23, 24

    Net worth: $70 million

    Niall Horan began his music career as a member of the boy band One Direction, formed in 2010 on the singing competition The X Factor. The group released five albums and went on to become one of the best-selling boy bands of all time. Thanks to that, Niall’s net worth sits at an estimated $70 million. He joined The Voice in season 23.

  • Reba McEntire

    Season 24

    Net worth: $95 million

    ‘The Queen of Country’ Reba McEntire served as a battle advisor on The Voice in seasons 1 and 8, and during season 23, McEntire acted as the overall Mega Mentor for every team. Come Season 24, she was appointed a coach. She’s valued at $95 million net worth, according to Celebrity Net Worth. In addition to her duties on The Voice, Reba played the role of Annie Oakley in the Broadway musical Annie Get Your Gun in 2001. The same year, The WB launched the TV series Reba, in which she starred.

  • Nick Jonas

    Seasons 18, 20, 24

    Net worth: $80 million

    Jonas joined as a Voice coach in 2020. He’s also one of the show’s youngest judges. He was 27 years old when he joined the show. But that doesn’t mean his net worth is something to scoff at. The Jonas Brothers member has a $80 million net worth, according to Celebrity Net Worth. Along with his work with the Jonas Brothers (with which he was nominated for two Grammys), Jonas also has two top 10 albums and a successful single, “Jealous.” He’s also partnered with brands like John Varvatos and Villa One.

  • Christina Aguilera

    Seasons 1 – 3, 5, 8, 10

    Net worth: $160 million

    Aguilera was one of the four OG coaches on The Voice when the show premiered in 2011 and was paid the most at the time. Her stock is reflected in her net worth, which is $160 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. The singer is the recipient of five Grammys and is behind hits like “Candyman,” “Beautiful” and “Hurt.”

  • Alicia Keys

    Seasons: 11 – 12, 14

    Net worth: $150 million

    Keys is often regarded as one of the best songwriters of the 21st century, so it should come as no surprise that she’s worth $150 million. The “Girl on Fire” singer is one of the most decorated women in Grammy history with 15 awards from 29 nominations. She’s also responsible for hits like “Fallin'”, “I Ain’t Got You” and “No One.”

  • John Legend

    Seasons 16 – 24

    Net worth: $100 million

    Legend has a $100 million net worth, according to Celebrity Net Worth. It shouldn’t be a surprise that the man is rich given his long music career. The “Ordinary People” singer has 11 Grammys and is known for hits like “All of Me” and “Love Me Now.” Did we mention he’s also an EGOT winner?

  • Gwen Stefani

    Seasons: 7, 9, 12, 17, 19, 22, 24

    Net worth: $160 million

    Gwen Stefani’s Voice career has been on and off, but fans think of her as a regular due to her relationship with Shelton. (Rumor has it that the two are both paid $13 million per season to play up their romance for the show.) Celebrity Net Worth reports that Stefani is worth $160 million, which accounts for her solo music and work with No Doubt. No Doubt has won two Grammys while Stefani has taken home three golden Gramophones on her own.

  • Adam Levine

    Seasons: 1 – 16

    Net worth: $160 million

    Before he left in season 16, Levine and Shelton were the longest-lasting Voice coaches. Per Celebrity Net Worth, the singer is worth a cool $160 million. As the lead singer of Maroon 5, Levine has won three Grammys and been nominated 10 times. He’s also been responsible for number-one hits like “Moves Like Jagger,” “Makes Me Wonder” and “One More Night.”

  • Blake Shelton

    Seasons: 1 to 23

    Net worth: $120 million

    Shelton was the longest-serving coach on The Voice. His tenure explains why he receives $13 million per season, which accounts for a lot of his net worth. Per Celebrity Net Worth, the country singer is worth $120 million. As we’ve explained before, The Voice has boosted Shelton’s career. After he started on show, each of his albums reached the top 10 on the Billboard Hot 100. He’s also been nominated for nine Grammys. He announced he was leaving the talent show in September 2023 so he could spend more time with family.

  • Miley Cyrus

    Seasons: 11, 13

    Net worth: $160 million

    Cyrus may be one of the youngest Voice coaches (she was only 23 when she joined as a judge in season 11), but she’s also one of the richest. She’s worth $160 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. As the star of Disney Channel’s Hannah Montana, Cyrus makes a ton of coin from those syndication checks. She also has her own music career, as the singer behind hits like “We Can’t Stop” and “Wrecking Ball.”

  • Usher

    Seasons: 4, 6

    Net worth: $180 million

    Usher may not have been on The Voice for that long, but his net worth is one of the highest on this list. Celebrity Net Worth reports that singer is worth $180 million. It shouldn’t come as a surprise to fans given that Usher was one of the hottest singers in the early to mid-2000s. The artist is responsible for songs like “Yeah!” and “My Boo.” He’s also won eight Grammys and discovered artists like Justin Bieber.

  • Ariana Grande

    Season 21

    Net worth: $240 million

    Ariana Grande is one of the most famous—and richest—singers to be a coach on The Voice. She joined for season 21 in 2021. According to Celebrity Net Worth, Grande is worth a massive $240 million as of 2023, which consists of money she made from her music career, her fragrance line, as well as partnerships with brands like Starbucks, American Express, Macy’s T-Mobile and Apple.

  • Pharrell

    Seasons: 7 – 10

    Net worth: $250 million

    Pharrell’s time on The Voice also wasn’t the longest, but the singer-writer-producer is worth an impressive $250 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. While Pharrell has his own hits like “Get Lucky” and “Happy,” the artist is best known in the music industry behind the scenes. The former Voice judge has produced songs like Migos’ “Stir Fry,” Ed Sheeran’s “Sing” and Robin Thicke’s “Blurred Lines,” which is why his net worth is so massive.

  • Shakira

    Seasons: 4, 6

    Net worth: $300 million

    And the richest Voice coach is…Shakira. The Latin-pop singer only judged on the show for two seasons, but we’re sure it’s because the series couldn’t afford her for more. According to Celebrity Net Worth, Shakira is worth $300 million. It’s easy to see why: She’s an international icon with hits like “Whenever, Wherever,” “Hips Don’t Lie” and “She Wolf.” She’s also won 12 Latin Grammys and three Grammys. She’s also one of the few people on this list who has performed the Super Bowl Half Time Show. Total boss.

  • Adam Levine

    Season 1 – 16

    Salary: $14 million per season

    Like Shelton, Levine was also an OG coach on The Voice. He left after season 16, but in 2016, The Wrap reported that he signed the same deal as Shelton to receive $13 million per season ($26 million a year.) However, after Levine quit The Voice, The Hollywood Reporter claimed that his salary was even higher at more than $14 million per season and close to $30 million a year.

  • Christina Aguilera

    Seasons 1 – 3, 5, 8, 10

    Salary: $12.5 million – $17 million per season

    The Wrap reported in 2014 that Aguilera made $17 million a season as a Voice coach in season 3 (much more than Levine and Shelton). The “Genie in a Bottle” singer quit after that season but returned in season 5, where she took a pay cut, according to The Wrap. For that season and onward, The Wrap reported that she earned $12.5 million a season.
