10 worst neighborhoods in Baltimore, Maryland as of 2024

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Wednesday, September 25, 2024

Baltimore is one of the biggest cities in the U.S. state of Maryland, with a population of over 580,000. The region is rich in history, as Paleo-Indians utilised it as a hunting ground. Baltimore’s culture is diverse as it is known for its impressive blend of beautiful features and challenging aspects like poverty and crime. So, what are the worst neighborhoods in Baltimore?

1. Cherry Hill

  • Violent crime per 100,000 people: 3,146
  • Population: 7,843

Cherry Hill is one of the southernmost regions of Baltimore, Maryland. The region ranks as the 10th worst neighbourhood in Baltimore, with a crime rate 3,146 per 100,000 people.

It is important to note that Cherry Hill is the home of Baltimore’s most significant public housing projects. There are also several low-income apartments throughout the community.

Cherry Hill is also home to the infamous MS-13 gang. Considering the high crime rate in this region, it is advisable to stay away if it is not necessary to visit it.

2. West Baltimore

  • Violent crime per 100,000: 3,197
  • Population: 52,160

West Baltimore is notorious for violent and drug-related crimes. Despite this negative face, the region is still renowned for its unique culture, endless streets of old Baltimore rowhouses with marble stoops and sprawling parklands. The crime rate here is 3,197 per 100,000.

There is a high difference between the daytime and night-time images as much crime happens at night. West Baltimore is heavily affected by poor education, high taxation, and poverty, making it a sound ground for crime to thrive.

3. Greenmount East

  • Violent crime per 100,000: 2,952
  • Population: 11,041

Greenmount East is one of the most dangerous streets in Baltimore, with a population of around 11,041 people and a crime rate of 2,952 per 100,000. The most common crime in Greenmount East is theft and burglary.

The BGF gang mainly controls the region. Although they are not as ruthless as the MS-13 gang, they are still dangerous as they have caused unrest, making it unsafe for residents and visitors.

According to the FBI, Greenmount East has 5,888 property crimes. Therefore, compared to its total of 2,952 violent crimes, the overall crime rate is 277% above the national average.

4. Fairfield District

  • Violent crime rate per 100,000: 2,974
  • Population: 530

Fairfield District has a deficient population. Even though the population is small, the crime rate is relatively high and stands at 2,974 per 100,000, which appears to be increasing.

Because of this high crime rate, it is one of the areas to avoid in downtown Baltimore. The region also doesn’t experience a large influx of tourists because of crime. According to FBI statistics, the crime rate in Fairfield District is 321% more than the national average.

5. Pulaski District

  • Violent crime rate per 100,000: 2,880
  • Population: 475

With such a small population, the crime rate (at 2,880) is relatively high in Pulaski District. Here, you are at an increased chance (1 out of 9) of becoming a victim of crime. The education system here is also lacklustre, with less than 70% of students graduating.

Interestingly, Pulaski District has a national aquarium that attracts tourists from other parts of the United States of America and other countries. The crime rate in Pulaski District is 3,075, which is above the national average. If you plan to visit the beautiful aquariums in the region, booking your accommodation outside this neighbourhood is best.

6. Brooklyn-Curtis Bay

  • Violent crimes per 100,000: 2,935
  • Population: 13,252

Although Brooklyn-Curtis Bay has some of the most affordable properties in Baltimore, the high crime rate might deter you from buying. The violent crime here stands at 2,935 per 100,000, which is 2,915 above the national average. You can get a house in Brooklyn-Curtis Bay at $66,000.

7. Madison-Eastend

  • Violent crimes per 100,000: 2,687
  • Population: 2,383

Madison East End ranks as the fourth worst neighbourhood in Baltimore. The region has a high unemployment rate of 114% above the national average. Therefore, you must avoid random burglary or theft while strolling these streets.

Even though Madison-Eastend ranks as one of the areas with crime in Baltimore, it is safer to visit than other regions, but it is not advisable to reside here.

8. Greater Rosemont

  • Violent crime per 100,000: 251
  • Population: 18,664

Greater Rosemont is a neighbourhood in west Baltimore, strategically located below North Avenue, above Franklintown Road, and East of Hilton Parkway. The region is so worn that it resembles a battlefield. There is also a low police presence.

9. Orangeville District

  • Violent crimes per 100,000: 1,818
  • Population: 2,235

Orangeville District’s crime rate is 1,818 per 100,000, 171% above the national average. Even though the crime rate doesn’t appear to be high, it is for a region with over 2,000 people.

10. Grove Park District

  • Violent crimes per 100,000: 1,541
  • Population: 1,872

Although Grove Park District is seen as one of Baltimore's most “family-friendly” regions, the high crime rate proves otherwise. On top of this, there is a massive lack of police presence, which increases the already high crime rate. This region ranks as the worst among the towns and ghettos in Baltimore.

What is the crime rate in Middle East Baltimore?

According to Property Club, Middle East Baltimore’s crime rate is 10,945 per 100,000, 445% higher than the Maryland state average. This rating makes the region one of the most violent in Baltimore.

Are there safe areas in Baltimore?

According to PODS, Baltimore has numerous safe neighbourhoods. Most crime is focused downtown in some high-poverty regions like Cherry Hill.
