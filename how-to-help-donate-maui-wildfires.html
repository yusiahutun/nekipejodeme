How to Help Those Affected by the Maui Wildfires

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, July 28, 2024

Residents, volunteers, and organizations are pooling their resources to help people affected by catastrophic wildfires on the Hawaiian island of Maui.

The fire has forced thousands of people out of their homes, destroyed the historic town of Lahaina (which served as the capital of the kingdom of Hawaii in the early 19th century), and left at least 115 people dead—a number that officials expect will rise dramatically as the full effects of the natural disaster are felt. Thousands of local residents and tourists are living in emergency shelters, and thousands were left without power in the immediate aftermath of the fires.

The Lahaina blaze is now deemed the deadliest wildfire in modern U.S. history, its death toll surpassing that of California’s Camp Fire, which killed 85 people in November 2018. Officials are asking Maui residents to provide DNA samples to help identify victims’ remains.

Locals have compiled a comprehensive listing of GoFundMes for affected families, community funds to donate to, donation drop-off points in Oahu, and small businesses that are supporting relief efforts. You can find the document here.

Bazaar.com has also put together a list of ways to support the wildfire victims in Hawaii right now. Scroll ahead to see how you can help.
