Unstitched: Skall Studio

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, July 12, 2024

When it comes to sustainable fashion, it can be hard to know where to start. To help, every month we will focus on a brand that knows exactly what it means to be a sustainable force for good in fashion today.

This month we’re getting to know Skall Studio, a Danish label that is redefining what it means to be conscious in fashion today.

Julie and Marie Skall launched their eponymous label Skall Studio back in 2014 and have since made a respected name for themselves within the sustainable fashion world. The small-scale Danish brand embodies all things capsule, with its clean, minimal aesthetic and pieces that can be styled with pretty much anything.

The Skall Sisters grew up in Denmark surrounded by nature on the coast, which has a huge influence on their approach to design and use of natural materials like Danish wool, linen, organic cotton and recycled cashmere. The label is known for its signature knitwear, made at one of the few remaining spinning mills in Denmark.

Courtesy of Skall Studio

Julie and Marie Skall

Its clothes are made to last, making the pieces an investment for your forever wardrobe; they stay clear from passing trends, can be worn by anyone and are designed to be passed down through generations.

Skall Studio focuses on three main areas: conscious production, animal welfare and natural materials. This hard work has seen the label recognised by sustainable experts over the years, leading to it becoming GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) certified. As a shopper, it can be hard to know which brands to trust or where to invest your money, so achieving these certifications as a brand is vital in fashion today.

Below, we meet the sister duo behind Skall Studio, who explain why brands must constantly evolve to be conscious, why less truly is more, and why research is key.

What makes a truly sustainable brand?

Julie Skall: "We constantly challenge ourselves. Being a conscious fashion brand, we feel obliged to always try to improve and push the boundaries. As well as this, we think conscious fashion is also about making styles that will last for a long time, both in terms of style and quality. At Skall Studio, we take the whole product life cycle into consideration when designing a new piece. When we put a new style into the world, we want it to last for as long as possible. Durability is key, so this is why we offer free repair on all our styles.

"We only work with natural materials like Danish wool, recycled cashmere, organic or recycled cotton as we believe synthetic materials have a negative impact on the environment. Being a conscious fashion brand obliges on different levels – it's also about ensuring good working conditions, both at the factories as well as in-house, and making sure that the animals you work with are thriving. You need to look at the full circle. Everything is connected."

What do you think needs to change in the fashion industry?

Marie Skall: "We need to change our way of consuming for a 'less and better is more' philosophy – as well as our way of designing. After all, it all starts in the design department – from material choices and sample requests to designs made to last."

What do you want to achieve personally with your brand, in terms of sustainability?

JK: "We want our customers to care for their products to make them last for as long as possible and to always consider material choices – as synthetic materials obviously have a negative impact on the environment – which is why we choose to only work with natural materials. Also, we hope to inspire our customers to live a life in balance and to reconsider how much they need to purchase and surround themselves with. This is often reflected in our blog Skall Letters."

Courtesy of Skall Studio

MK: "We also hope to inspire our customers to value craftsmanship. We have worked with one of the last remaining spinning mills and one of the last remaining knitting factories in Denmark for many years and for our spring/summer 2023 collection we have looked into Indian craftsmanship as well – from natural dyes and handloom textiles made in India to block print inspiration and Kantha stitching. Craftsmanship will always be essential to Skall Studio, in Denmark as well as all over the world. It's all about going back to the roots and we believe this is a good contrast to mass production and an opportunity to take a step in a better direction."

Do you think our attitudes to fashion have changed since you started your brand? If so, how?

MK: "Yes, absolutely. When we founded Skall Studio in 2014, we were among the first Danish fashion brands to talk about conscious fashion. In the beginning, only a few of our customers were interested in sustainability and conscious fashion so it was not something that we talked much about. Today our customers want to know as much as possible about our products and how they are made. So, obviously so much has happened since we started out. We feel grateful for that."

What advice would you give to those wanting to make their business sustainable?

JK: "It's much better to do something today than wait. Even though sometimes it only feels like small baby steps, they are still steps in the right direction. Don't talk, take action."

What’s the smallest change a consumer could make to become more eco-conscious?

JK: "In our opinion, the best thing a consumer can do is to avoid synthetic materials and to buy styles made to last, both in terms of quality and design. In the end, it all comes down to how much we consume."

What’s the biggest misconception about sustainable clothing and what would you say to counteract this?

MK: "For the last couple of years, we have seen a tremendous focus on reselling platforms. In our opinion, too many brands have been obsessed with this idea without considering how many clothes they put into the world. In fact, the reselling platform often functions as a way of justifying clothes made of bad quality, as you can always cover your back by offering the reselling opportunity. The reselling platform can be a good thing, however, it must never function as an opportunity to put even more clothes into the world."

Where do you turn to when you feel confused about sustainability and need more detail?

JK: "Research is a core value at Skall Studio. We put a lot of effort into digging into sustainability issues – and we never turn away from difficulties, we try to solve them. Having said so, we have some very dedicated people that we can turn to when we need further info or a second opinion. We are very grateful for these connections."
