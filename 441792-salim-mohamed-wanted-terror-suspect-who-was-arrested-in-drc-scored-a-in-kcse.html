Salim Mohamed: Wanted Terror Suspect Who Was Arrested in DRC Scored A- in KCSE

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, August 19, 2024
  • Salim Mohamed was nabbed in DRC, a couple of months after he was declared a wanted terror criminal by DCI
  • Before involving himself with terror activities, Mohamed was a bright student who even scored an A-minus in his 2014 KCSE
  • It is believed he became acquainted with terrorism activities through the internet

On Saturday, January 29, 28-year-old Salim Mohamed, a wanted terror suspect in Kenya was arrested in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

He was in the company of two people and were on their way to South Africa when he was apprehended by the Armed Forces of DR Congo.

How he transformed from being an intelligent A-student to a suspected terrorist still baffles his Mombasa-based family who said he performed well in his national exams, Daily Nation reported.

The 28-year-old passed his Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) in 2010 and in 2014, he emerged as the best student at AgaKhan High School in Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams.

He scored an A-minus.

Prayerful young man

All this while, his family said he maintained a normal demeanour of a young, intelligent man who also loved praying.

However, he started changing after his KCSE exams; he received a calling letter to join the Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology but he declined the offer.

After persuasion from the family, he agreed to join the Technical University of Mombasa to pursue an engineering course.

His father would even later convince him to study in Turkey but he was later deported back to Kenya on suspicion of terror links.

Terror links through the internet

It is believed that he joined terror groups through the internet. His friends would often see him with computers, moving from one cyber to another.

An investigation by police officers showed that computers aided Mohamed in the discovery of a terror cell affiliated to ISIS in Kwale where he would secretly visit from time to time.

As TUKO.co.ke reported, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations put a KSh 50 million bounty on Mohamed's head in November 2021.

The agency said he was out on bond over terrorism charges and is suspected to have joined the Islamic State terrorist group in Mozambique.

Salim hails from Mombasa and was previously linked to various criminal activities in the country and parts of the south coast.
