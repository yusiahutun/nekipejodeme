'Australia's best beach': Travellers rave about Wharton Beach near Esperance, WA

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, August 9, 2024

An untouched bay with a five-star travel rating has been hailed 'Australia's most beautiful beach' by bloggers and tourists.

Wharton Bay in Cape Le Grand, 100km east of the wheat belt town of Esperance, is best described as one of Western Australia's hidden gems, captivating visitors lucky enough to find it with its pristine white sand and sparkling turquoise water.

Photos of the beach uploaded to Instagram by Sydney photographer, Adam Rikys, have sparked stunned responses, with travel influencer Kendall Baggerly calling it 'one of the best beaches in Australia'.

Four-wheel drives have direct access to the beach, which boasts some of the best swimming, surfing, diving and fishing in the region.

TripAdvisor ranks it 22nd out of 61 'things to do' near Esperance, after Cape Le Grand National Park and more famous beaches such as Lucky Bay and Twilight Bay.

But travellers insist Wharton Beach deserves a place higher up the list.

The beach has attracted rave reviews online, with many calling it the most picturesque they have ever visited.

'This is hands down the PRETTIEST beach I've ever seen,' one woman wrote.

She said the water is a brilliant shade of turquoise and the sand is so fine it 'squeaks' as you walk on it.

'This beach is the closest thing to paradise. It's definitely a hidden gem!' she added.
