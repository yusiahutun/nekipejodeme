Kylie Jenner mocked after Kardashian fans spot star's new 'collab' with major store that looks 'boot

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, June 2, 2024

KYLIE Jenner's new collaboration has faced massive criticism online after it was seen at a retail store.

Kylie, 25, appears to have a new collaboration with Bratz Dolls, which are plastic toy dolls.

The collaboration is called Kylie x Bratz, and it features 14 different plastic figurines that appear to be about one inch tall.

The figures even include a tiny version of one of the Kardashian's Italian Greyhound pups.

All of the figurines are packaged individually in mystery boxes.

The packaging is bright pink and purple, and the box says, "Collect Kylie's Bratzified looks!" in English, German, and Spanish.

The strangest aspect of the toy is centered around the photo of Kylie that is used on the box.

Her photo appears to be a selfie taken off of social media, not professionally taken.

The original poster of the Reddit thread said they found the product at a Target in San Antonio, Texas.

QUESTIONABLE DECISION

Followers of The Kardashians on Reddit had a lot to say about these toys and their packaging.

HYPOCRISY ON BLAST

Kylie was slammed after making comments about other people's editing habits on the most recent episode of the family's Hulu show.

She was joined by her older sisters Khloe Kardashian, 39, and Kourtney Kardashian, 44.

As Kylie did her makeup, Kourtney said: “I was thinking about the beauty standards in the world today.”

Kylie replied: “I just feel like we have a huge influence, and what are we doing with our power?

"I see so many young girls on the internet that are fully editing. And I went through that stage too but I feel like I’m in a better place now. But others can instill those insecurities in you.”

In a confessional, the mother of two added: “We all have our little things. It’s normal to be insecure sometimes. But I always just remember being the most confident kid in the room.

"I always loved myself and I still love myself. One of the biggest misconceptions about me is that I was this insecure child and I got so much surgery to change my whole face which is false. I’ve only gotten fillers. So I don’t want that to be a part of my story."

She concluded: "I will always want everyone to just love themselves.”

One person reposted the scene to Reddit, where many called out Kylie for slamming editing despite being caught altering her own photos.
