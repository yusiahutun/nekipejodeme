20 distressed locs ideas for short, medium, and long hair

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, May 18, 2024

People love looking great. Stylists have been on top of their game to bring the necessary changes and variety to Afro hairstyles. You have probably come across various locs styles over the years, but the latest look capturing people's attention is the distressed locs hairstyle. Everyone wants it. So, what are the best styles to go for?

If you are searching for a new look, something funky and fresh, then look no further than the distressed look. As the name suggests, the hairstyle is distressed and looks natural, giving you fantastic comfort. Another remarkable thing is that they are versatile and fun. You can wear them for a long time. But first, you must choose the most suitable distressed faux locs, perfect for your unique hair type.

Whether you have short, medium, or long hair, there is a distressed locs look for you. When perfectly installed, people can hardly tell the difference between your natural hair and the distressed locs because of how it looks. So, what are the best distressed locs on natural hair?

1. Thin distressed locs

The thin distressed locs hairstyle is perfect if you have fine and flat hair. The style is a creative way of adding volume as you take small sections and braid or wrap them. The result is a hairstyle with a lot of units hence the volume. In every section, you add the hair and wrap your braids with that hair, adding volume to it and creating thicker, more volumised braids.

2. Distressed butterfly locs

If you are looking for a perfect balance between bouncy and in place, you have the distressed butterfly locs. It is one of the best distressed locs hairstyles in the market, perfect for casual and official functions.

It is a funky groove that allows excellent movement to your hair. That is why teenagers adore it, but even you can wear it. Butterfly locs create sufficient volume in your hair, making it look less stiff and tight.

3. Distressed faux butterfly locs

If you are looking for a hairstyle that looks fancy on virtually every head, you can go for the distressed faux butterfly locs. You only need to purchase your distressed faux hair from the store and start the work. Get a good stylist to style it nicely. You can also do it by yourself by following a few simple steps.

You can start by parting your hair and braiding the roots. First, ensure you moisturise since you will have the style for some time and don't want an itchy scalp. Next, get your faux locs and attach them to your natural hair from the roots. Then, wrap them around your braids. Finally, utilise your comb to make a loose wrap at least three to two times per loc to give it a distressed natural appearance.

4. Bohemian distressed faux locs

People who still love the bohemian look don't have to let it go since they are going for distressed locs. You can have the perfect combination when you go for the bohemian distressed faux locs. They are popular because of the excellent they look.

5. Distressed locs bob

If you have short hair, the distressed locs bob is your ideal look. It is a fun, casual, outgoing style you can settle on if you search for something less stressful and easier to manage. The look has become common among school-going children as it is easy to create. You need a side or middle part to style your hair, and you're done.

6. Blonde distressed locs

There are two ways to get the blonde distressed locs style. First, you can procure blonde locs and wrap or crotchet them to your hair. Another way is bleaching your hair. Of course, the buying route is ideal if you don't want to mess up your hair or change its colour. The style is incredible as it gives an illusion of a brighter skin tone against bleached locs.

7. Burgundy distressed locs

If you are tired of the same style and are short of ideas to revamp your hairstyle, it is probably about time you try another colour, and burgundy is one of the most versatile. In addition, the burgundy distressed locs bring a romantic vibe to your locs as they are brighter and livelier.

The colour looks great on everyone, which is why it is an excellent choice for someone who has just started venturing into the colour spectrum of hairstyles.

8. Brown distressed locs

Brown is another great colour to have on your distressed locs. It fits well with most people's styles. And it would be perfect if you have a bronze or chocolate skin tone.

10. Ombre distressed locs

You don’t have to let go of the ombre effect when wearing distressed locs. You can still have the ombre look, smoothly merging two tones. Once you have installed your distressed locs, you can have it styled the half-up, half-down way. You don't have to let your hair look messy. To create this style, ascertain that your locs are long enough to assemble the perfect middle bun and have enough volume to slide down towards your shoulders.

How do you create distressed faux locs crochet?

You can procure a bunch of the appropriate hair from the market and start crocheting them onto your roots. Of course, you will need a good pair of hairbrushes and some clips to actualise the look.

How do you make faux locs look distressed?

You will need edge control, scissors, a tail comb, or any comb to section your hair. On top of this, you need around three packs of hair, or you can utilise faux locs you have on hand.

You will start by prepping the faux locks by splitting them into three sections. Doing this is excellent, especially if you are a beginner or want your loc to lay seamlessly.

Create a foundation for the distressed fox loc, then attach the loc to your hair. To wrap your hair, you must wrap it two to three times at the roots to secure the loc and start moving further down. Shift the hair up after every three to four rounds.

What hair do you use for distressed faux locs?

The secret to creating tension-free locs is the type of hair used. Therefore, you can go for a human hair blend, water wave passion twist hair, afro kinky hair, or crotchet faux locs.

What kind of hair do you use for distressed locs?

Marley hair is the best for distressed locs. You can also settle for freetress bohemian braids or freetress water wave hair to wrap the locs. The most important thing is to use hair that will give you that distressed feel, is strong and can quickly be covered.

What is the difference between distressed locs and butterfly locs?

The most significant difference between distressed locs and butterfly locs is the curly loops present in the butterfly loc. The curly loops give the butterfly loc its signature style, a perfect mix between goddess locs and passion twists.
