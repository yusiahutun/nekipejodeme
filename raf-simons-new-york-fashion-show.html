Raf Simons Seems Really Excited to Show in New York

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, March 10, 2024

Last night, Raf Simons presented his Fall 2017 collection at New York Fashion Week: Men's, marking his first time ever showing in the Big Apple. After traditionally hosting runway shows in Paris, Simons made his NYFW debut with a clear tribute to his new city.

Perhaps the dead giveaway were the sweaters emblazoned with an abstract iteration of the tourist-sought "I ♥ NY" logo. (Simons' rendition scrapped the letter I and the top half of the heart.) Expect to see these all over the street style scene in the coming seasons―maybe even with those clustered beaded chokers, too.

Getty Images

Getty Images

Other cropped styles simply read "I love you" instead, further proving the collection's status as a love letter to New York.

Getty Images

The tribute also manifested on a smaller scale than the graphic knits. Vests and coats were cinched at the waist with duct tape-esque belts that were printed with the same "I ♥ NY" emblem.

"I see this as a city with incredible energy, incredible inspiration, incredible people," Simons told WWD. "I always pick up [inspiration] from where I live and there is an incredible freedom in the street here."

Getty Images

Simons received a warm welcome to the city, proving his affectoin for it is mutual. New York-based designers like Joseph Altuzarra, Narciso Rodriguez and Proenza Schouler's Lazaro Hernandez and Jack McCollough attended the presentation. Fashionable New Yorkers, like Neil Patrick Harris and Harlem native A$AP Rocky, were among the stylish crowd in tow. Unsurprisingly, the show was well-received on social media.

This is only the beginning of the Belgian designer's New York escapades, and the next one is soon to follow. He will be presenting his debut collection as creative director of Calvin Klein at NYFW later this month.
