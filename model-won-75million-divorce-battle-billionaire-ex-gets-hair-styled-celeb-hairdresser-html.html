Model who won 75MILLION in divorce battle with billionaire ex gets hair styled by celeb hairdresse

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, April 21, 2024

A former Pirelli calendar model who was awarded £75million in a divorce settlement from her Saudi sheikh ex-husband has showcased her post-lockdown hairstyle thanks to a celebrity-loved hairdresser.

Ex-supermodel Christina Estrada displayed her glamorous curled, brunette locks in a snap with hairdresser Michael Charalambous, based in Chelsea and favoured by famous faces such as Claudia Winkleman, as salons opened today for the first time in three months.

Proving she's still living a glamorous lifestyle, Ms Estrada donned a stylish, figure-hugging white and green dress with a plunging V-neck for her photoshoot.

The American, who has been based in the UK since 1988, was married to Saudi billionaire Sheikh Walid Juffali for 13 years before he took a second wife and divorced Ms Estrada without her knowledge, as Saudi law allows polygamy.

She was awarded a £53million cash settlement in July 2016 with her lawyers saying the payout was worth £75million when her assets were taken into account.

The settlement, which was awarded at the High Court in London, was one of the largest in British legal history, and included cars, jewellery and property around the world.

Businessman Dr Juffali died from cancer aged 61 in July 2016, just weeks after the couple's divorce hearing.

During the divorce hearing, Ms Estrada argued in the witness box that she was entitled to an amount which would allow her to seek to maintain her ultra-wealthy lifestyle. 

She told the family court judge: 'I am Christina Estrada. I was a top, international model. I have lived this life. This is what I am accustomed to.

'It is difficult to convey the extraordinary level of luxury and opulence we were fortunate enough to enjoy.' 

Proving she's still living a glamorous lifestyle, Ms Estrada (pictured centre) donned a stylish, figure-hugging white and green dress with a plunging V-neck for her photoshoot

The settlement, which was awarded at the High Court in London, was one of the largest in British legal history, and included cars, jewellery and property around the world. Pictured, Ms Estrada in 2017, left, and in 2018, right

The case astonished many after Ms Estrada claimed her 'needs' included £1million for clothes, £40,000 for fur coats, £109,000 for haute couture dresses and £21,000 for shoes every year.

She also said she needed enough from her ex-husband to afford a luxury home in London worth about £60million, a £4.4million house in the country at Henley, as well as £495,000 for five cars - three in London and two in the US. 

Judges heard that the pair met and married in 2001 and separated in 2013.

Dr Juffali, a businessman, had mounted a diplomatic immunity defence after Ms Estrada, who is based in London, made a claim on his estimated £4billion fortune.

During the divorce hearing, Ms Estrada (pictured outside London's High Court in 2016) argued in the witness box that she was entitled to an amount which would allow her to seek to maintain her ultra-wealthy lifestyle

He argued that Ms Estrada was prevented from suing him in an English court because he had immunity through his post as permanent representative to the International Maritime Organisation for the Caribbean island of Saint Lucia. 

But a High Court judge ruled against him and Court of Appeal judges dismissed an appeal. 

Marriage troubles surfaced after Dr Juffali's decision in 2012 to marry a 25-year-old Lebanese model, the mother of his two youngest children, while still married to Ms Estrada, as Saudi law allows polygamy.

The sheikh then divorced Ms Estrada in Saudi Arabia in 2014 under Islamic law without her knowledge.

THE 'MAGICAL' LIFESTYLE OF THE MODEL AND HER SHEIKH HUSBAND: HOW THE GIRL FROM A 'MIDDLE CLASS' BACKGROUND SPENT MILLIONS 

Former supermodel Christina Estrada described her ultra-wealthy lifestyle during her marriage to a rich Saudi businessman as 'magical'.

The backdrop to family life over the years was one of opulent houses, yachts and private jets, with the couple enjoying what a High Court judge said was an 'exorbitant standard of living'.

They travelled internationally in their quest to find works works of art and antiques to furnish their vast London home.

Ms Estrada was often given gifts of expensive jewellery and lavish parties were held to celebrate birthdays.

Their homes included a substantial chalet which was built on a plot of land in Gstaad, Switzerland. Several floors were constructed underground to house a swimming pool and parking for up to 16 vehicles.

Quartz was imported from Afghanistan to create one particular wall in the chalet - and walls in an underground spa were fashioned in amethyst, which is Ms Estrada's birthstone.

Her 46th birthday was celebrated at the chalet in 2010 when she was presented with diamond jewellery worth around one million dollars.

For her 50th birthday, a party at a luxury resort in Abu Dhabi was attended by 200 guests and the cost of the firework display alone was said to have been one million dollars.
