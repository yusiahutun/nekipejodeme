Is Bill Nye the Science Guy a real scientist?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, January 1, 0001

MANY fans may be surprised to learn that their favorite TV scientist is technically not a scientist but an engineer-turned-comedian-turned science whizz.

Although he studied engineering at Cornell University and went on to work for Boeing, much of his science is self-taught.

Is Bill Nye the Science Guy a real scientist?

The answer to this one gets complicated and depends on who one asks.

A typical scientist does research which they contribute to universities or private labs.

Despite having six honorary doctorate degrees and a Bachelor's degree of Science in engineering from an Ivy League school, Nye hasn't contributed research to institutions like most typical scientists.

Instead, he worked for Boeing, where he invented a plane part used in the aerospace field today.

While working for Boeing, Nye night-lined as a stand-up comic.

Bill Nye the Science Guy actually began as a comedic bit before it was developed into an educational television series in the 1990s for kids.

What is Bill Nye doing these days?

Today, Nye serves as a co-chair for the March for Science and is an activist for climate change reform.

He also has an upcoming series on Peacock called The End is Nye, which is all about natural disasters.

He is interviewed on the news often about such topics, which is why he's under fire for not being a real scientist.

However, Bill Nye continues to spark people's interest in science.

So while he may not personally be a scientist, many argue that he has done a lot for the scientific community.

What is Bill Nye's net worth?

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Nye has a net worth of around $8million.

This wealth has accumulated from patents, various TV shows, and movie appearances over the years.
