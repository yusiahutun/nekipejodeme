Natural looking eyelash extensions - Edy London lashes review

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, April 16, 2024

As transformative as a spray tan and as addictive as gel manicures, eyelash extensions are one of those life-changing beauty treatments.

To me, an investment in extensions is a saving on mascara (its application and removal I find a chore). But in addition to their routine-streamlining attributes, eyelash extensions can claim much more: an instant wide-eyed appearance – delivering a dose of feeling ‘done’ – the second you clamber out of bed, plus smudge-free eyelids from zero mascara transfer which, in my case, can accumulate throughout the day thanks to my hooded eyelids.

As with tans and manis, my aim with lash extensions is to maintain an enhanced realness, as opposed to an obviously fake look. Therefore, I opt for ‘classic lashes’ where one extension is applied to one natural lash, in favour of those marketed as ‘Russian layering’, ‘volume’ or ‘3D lashes’ where more than one extension is attached per natural lash. For the last six years Edyta Kurowska (known as Edy London, who is based at Beauty Club London) has been my technician of choice. As seen via her work showcased on Instagram – modelled by the famous such as Ellie Goulding, Lily Allen and Trinny Woodall ­– her extensions aren’t instantly detectable. Instead, you see her clients’ perfectly defined, bright eyes, framed by long, dark lashes.

Having worked in the lash game for over 15 years, it really helps that Kurowska’s original trade is make-up artistry. This training and experience means that she understands face and eye shapes, allowing her to tailor creations to one’s individual look, with the resulting effect of ‘you but better’. Adapting the colour, curl, length and thickness to each individual, she will “take into account the consideration the face shape, the space between brows and lash lines, the natural lash condition, and whether anything needs enhancing or correcting,” for example.

Over a 60-to-75-minute appointment, using very fine individual silk and synthetic mink lashes, Kurowska employs her “one-to-one technique”. This method, signature to classic lash extensions, not only means more natural results, but it prevents any damage of the real lashes. She explains, “it lets the extensions fall out naturally without showing any gaps,” adding that “it’s a very low-maintenance technique.”

Edy London

Indeed, her lashes last much longer on me than previous extensions I’ve tried, given that I only lose one at a time in line with my natural lash life cycle. Even a month after application I retain a few evenly spread extensions, and I enhance any lost volume with a quick coat of regular mascara – which works well for me.

Of course, to maintain a uniform look I could have infills, which can be done every two-to-three weeks (with a 40 minute appointment), but inevitably time slips away with me.

I now consider Kurowska the best in the business for eyelash extensions in London, but whether you can visit her in the capital or not, below are some of both our top tips for healthy, natural-looking lash extensions that last.

What to look and ask for to achieve natural-looking eyelash extensions:

  • A lash technician with a make-up background (as opposed to experience in brows, or as a general beauty therapist) is ideal, given that they will likely understand eye shapes and depths. Find out how much experience they have in the specific area of lashes. They should have a qualification from recognised brands such as Lash Perfect, Nova Lash or Nouveau Lashes.
  • Do your research and look at imagery of their work to see if it’s to your taste. Instagram and TikTok are your best spying grounds. At an appointment, explain how you like your lashes to look: thick, curly, long, defined. Explain what you generally look for in a mascara.
  • Ask your potential technician what kind of extensions they use. The most common are mink, silk and synthetic (I, personally, don't fancy mink fur near my eyes). The length and thickness is also important for a realistic look; with natural being Kurowska’s specialism, she uses lengths up to 13mm and 0.10mm thick. (Lash extensions can go up to 0.25 in thickness and 20mm in length, which she advises against for your lash health.)
  • Inquire about the adhesive used, too. Kurowska uses hypoallergenic medical grade glues that are fast-drying and long-lasting. She says to be cautious about the use of LED to bond lash extensions in place of traditional adhesive. With no disclosure of the wavelength used in this technique, it might pose safety risks. “There are a lot of published studies on potential eye damage due to bright light exposure directly to the eye, including retinal damage and age-related degeneration, which isn’t reversible. Also, the skin around eye area is very delicate, and is high risk area for a skin cancer.” In the Instagram video below, Kurowska and cosmetic doctor Dr Sophie Shotter discuss the dangers of LED lashes.

How to make your eyelash extensions last:

  • Always follow the aftercare advice. Don’t get your eyes wet for 12-to-24 hours after application of the lashes. Obviously, don’t pick or pull your lashes.
  • Avoid sleeping on your front. If you do squash your lashes by mistake, brush them gently with a clean mascara brush or spoolie to help to help readjust the lash direction.
  • When swimming with lash extensions, if they get wet with pool or sea water, rinse them with some fresh water straight afterwards if possible.
  • If you need to remove eyeliner and eye shadow, use an oil-free make-up remover (oil will soften the adhesive and therefore loosen the lashes). Kurowska recommends Bioderma Sensibio H2O micellar cleanser. Or try Revitalash Micellar Water Lash Wash, which is safe for use with lash extensions. Avoid using wipes and cotton wool, as the fibres can tangle in your lashes. Use reusable rounds (the Garnier Micellar Reusable Make-Up Remover Eco Pads are perfect) or a cotton bud – avoiding the lash areas – instead.
  • When it comes to eye make-up, opt for cream eye shadows over powders, as powder particles can collect in the lashes. Try Trinny London Eye2Eye or the Vieve Eye Wands.
  • If you need to introduce mascara as your extensions fall out, Kurowska recommends a very light coating of the the Clinique High Impact mascara. I love lightly using the Lancôme Hypnôse.
  • Don't worry about lash extensions damaging your natural lashes – if applied correctly by a qualified technician and then allowed to shed naturally, this shouldn't be the case. However, you can support your natural lash growth while wearing extensions, with a product like The Ordinary Multi-Peptide Lash & Brow Serum, which can be used alongside lash extensions.
