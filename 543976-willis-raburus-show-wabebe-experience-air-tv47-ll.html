Willis Raburu's Show Wabebe Experience Taken Off Air at TV47: &quot;We'll Be Back&quot;

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, September 11, 2024
  • Fans of Willis Raburu and MC Gogo's Wabebe Experience on TV47 were puzzled after the show failed to air
  • The former Citizen TV newscaster posted an update on social media, announcing that the show was on a break
  • Many fans said they hoped that the show would return, as it made them look forward to Fridays

Kenyans have raised questions after Willis Raburu announced that his show on TV47 is off-air.

What happened to Wabebe Experience?

In a Saturday, April 6 post, the journalist, who left Citizen TV in August 2023, announced that Wabebe Experience, which he hosts alongside MC Gogo, is taking a break.

Raburu thanked fans for their unwavering support and assured them that the show would be back on the air.

“We want to announce that Wabebe Experience will take a season break. We sincerely thank our viewers for their continued support and enthusiasm. Rest assured, we are working diligently behind the scenes to return bigger and better than ever!Stay tuned for updates on our return! We love you and appreciate you so much,” he wrote on X.

In the comments, one fan questioned what informed the break, and Raburu responded by saying:

"Don't panic."

How many journalists moved to TV47?

In other news, Kenyans witnessed a migration of top media personalities from legacy media stations to Cape Media.

The company launched a massive recruitment drive for TV 47, Radio 47 and their online news platform.

TUKO.co.ke listed some renowned media practitioners, including Willis Raburu and Grace Kuria, who joined them.

The others include Alex Mwakideu and the trio of Emmanuel Mwashumbe, Mbaruk Mwalimu and Billy Miya from Radio Maisha.
