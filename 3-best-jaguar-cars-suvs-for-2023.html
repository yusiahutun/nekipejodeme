3 Best Jaguar Cars &amp; SUVs for 2023

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, August 30, 2024

Jaguar has a reputation for creating cars that are serene and comfortable for everyday driving while also being capable of exciting drivers during more spirited drives. Its 2023 lineup is no exception.

The XF coupe and convertible offer the brand’s signature blend of striking contemporary styling and pulse-quickening performance. This year, it gains a new infotainment system called Pivi Pro.  

Jaguar servicing near your in North York or in neighboring cities can ensure your vehicle’s engines are running smoothly and efficiently, whether you have a model from the 2023 lineup or one of the brand’s older classics. 

1. Jaguar XF 

If you’re looking for a luxurious, upscale sedan with a lot of standard features but don’t want to pay top dollar, the Jaguar XF is worth a look. It offers a comfortable ride and plenty of smart driver-assistance technologies to make every drive easier. 

Its spacious cabin is also filled with quality materials, and it’s loaded with standard amenities for both the driver and passengers. For example, it has plenty of nooks and crannies for storage and includes two cupholders up front and mediocre pockets in the doors. It also has a handy rear armrest cubby and glove compartment for more storage. 

The XF’s massive touchscreen runs Jaguar’s friendly and easy-to-use Pivi Pro infotainment system. It also boasts Apple CarPlay and Android Auto connectivity and supports Amazon Alexa voice control. Other standard features include keyless entry, dual-zone climate control, and power-adjustable front seats. 

The XF’s performance is impressive, too. It can reach 60 mph in about five seconds and provides balletic handling thanks to its nimble size and light weight. If you go for the supercharged XF S, you’ll get 380 horsepower that’s sure to thrill. Its agile nature is made all the more impressive when you consider that it was almost 140 lbs lighter than its German rivals, like the BMW 5 Series and Mercedes-Benz E-Class. 

2. Jaguar F-Pace 

The 2023 Jaguar F-Pace is a five-seat compact luxury SUV that’s fun to drive on twisty roads. It has the agility of a sports car and is a strong competitor against rivals like the BMW X3, Lexus NX, Lincoln Corsair, Porsche Macan, and Volvo XC60. The F-Pace’s quick reflexes and athletic road manners are made even more spirited by its range of powerful engines. The base 246-horsepower turbo four-cylinder is standard, while the top-of-the-line R-Dynamic S offers a mellifluous 395-horsepower mild-hybrid inline-six. If you want more oomph, there’s also a 550-horsepower V-8 in the range-topping SVR. 

The F-Pace is available with a variety of luxury and technology features, including a 11.4-inch curved HD touchscreen with the Pivi Pro infotainment system. The system supports Amazon Alexa capabilities, which integrates natural voice recognition with key features of the infotainment system. You can use voice commands to access functions like navigation, media playback, phone calls, and more. 

The upscale cabin in the F-Pace feels high-quality and well-crafted, with plenty of soft-touch materials. It’s also spacious and quiet, though you’ll have to pay extra for many luxury amenities like adaptive cruise control. Those who want to up the premium feel can add wood trim and leather upholstery, while a panoramic roof, power recline feature in the second row of seats, and interactive driver display are available as optional upgrades. 

3. Jaguar E-PACE 

Jaguar has entered the crowded luxury compact SUV market with its svelte, agile E-Pace. Available since 2017, this small crossover offers a bit of everything: European style, sporty driving dynamics, and decent tech and safety features. 

The E-Pace is a quick and agile car that’s fun to drive, even when it’s just cruising around town. It has a lively turbocharged four-cylinder engine, good handling, and plenty of standard safety features. Plus, it’s a good choice for families thanks to its roomy interior and plentiful rear legroom. 

However, it’s not without its flaws. It’s fairly heavy, lacking the aluminum construction that Jaguar uses on its XE and F-Pace sedans. This makes the base 2.0-liter diesel manual feel ponderous and slow to build momentum, while the more potent diesel automatic model is thirsty for fuel. 

Fortunately, the E-Pace’s standard Pivi Pro infotainment system is easy to use, and the automaker’s Siri Eyes Free feature lets you interact with your phone’s apps through its touchscreen. Other standard equipment includes grained leather seats, heated 12-way power-adjustable front seats, a Meridian sound system, and wireless device charging. It also comes with keyless entry and ignition, a rearview camera, and adaptive cruise control with stop-and-go functionality.
