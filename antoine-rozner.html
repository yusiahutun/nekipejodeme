Antoine Rozner Bio, Age, Height, Parents, Girlfriend, Net Worth

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, March 17, 2024

Antoine Rozner is a French pro golfer who competes on the PGA as well as on European Tour, where he has three titles under his belt, including the Golf in Dubai Championship in 2020 and the Commercial Bank Qatar Masters in 2021. He also has two victories on the Challenge Tour, the lower level of the tour. He played for France at the postponed 2020 Olympics in Tokyo, Japan.

Rozner secured his third European Tour title with a five-stroke win at the AfrAsia Bank Mauritius Open recently. He shot a final round 66 to finish at 25-under-par, five strokes ahead of his nearest rival, Thomas Detry of Belgium. Rozner became the first Frenchman to win three times on the European Tour since Victor Dubuisson in 2015. Rozner is currently ranked 63rd in the Official World Golf Ranking and 24th in the Race to Dubai standings.

Early Life

Rozner was born in Paris, France, on February 12th, 1993. He comes from a golf family; his mother, Laurence, was a good player and got Antoine started at a young age. Like his older brother Olivier, Rozner is a pro golfer. He studied and played golf at the University of Missouri–Kansas City from 2012-16, winning six tournaments. He also won all his four matches for Europe at the 2016 Arnold Palmer Cup and played for France at the 2016 Eisenhower Trophy.

Antoine Rozner Career

After the 2016 Eisenhower Trophy, Rozner turned professional. He started playing on the Alps Tour in 2017 and secured a spot on the Challenge Tour for 2018 by finishing third in the rankings. Antoine won his first two titles on the Challenge Tour in 2019, at the Challenge de España and the Prague Golf Challenge. He finished eighth on the Road to Mallorca to secure his card for the 2020 European Tour.

He claimed his maiden European Tour title at the 2020 Golf in Dubai Championship, where he shot a final round 64 to overtake Andy Sullivan. Rozner set a record for the quickest French double on Tour when he won the 2021 Commercial Bank Qatar Masters by one stroke, sinking a 60-foot birdie putt on the final hole. He added his third title at the 2022 AfrAsia Bank Mauritius Open, where he dominated the field with a five-shot win.
