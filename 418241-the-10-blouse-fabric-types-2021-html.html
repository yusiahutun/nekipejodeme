The 10 best blouse fabric types you should try out in 2021

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, April 15, 2024

According to Coco Chanel, fashion changes, but style endures. A blouse is a timeless fashion piece that has remained a must-have for ladies across all age groups. Ladies can pick from a wide selection of blouse fabric types depending on the occasion or the outfit they are wearing.

The blouse was invented in the 1890s and was not one of the fashionable garments for ladies. Its primary purpose was to cover the undergarments and absorb bodily soil. Today, the garment comes in various blouse fabric types for a wide range of outfits. So, which fabric is best for a blouse?

10 blouse fabric types you can try out in 2021

As the fashion industry has continued to evolve, fashion designers have used numerous fabrics to develop countless creations. There are different types of fabrics suited for ladies' blouses, all dependent on your taste and preference. Here is a list of types of blouse fabrics with pictures:

1. Silk

Silk is the go-to fabric if you want a smooth and unique fabric with a beautiful lustre. Silk blouse materials are eye-catching and come in different types, including chiffon, organza, charmeuse and crepe.

Each of them come from a different silkworm, and all carry various features that make them perfect for specific garments. The blouse design you wish to achieve should inform your fabric of choice.

Silk blouses are ideal for outdoor social events. You can wear them with a saree, denim, cotton or even silk pants. Peplum silk blouses go well with denim skinny jeans and block heels For the official look, you can wear your silk top with an official skirt or trouser suit.

One disadvantage of silk blouses is that they do not absorb sweat. However, you can get armpit pads that help to absorb the excess sweat, leaving your blouse looking elegant throughout the day.

2. Cotton

Cotton is another timeless and fancy blouse material that is a go-to for many women. It is a readily available and natural woven fabric that is one of the best to make blouses. In addition, cotton is ideal for all weather conditions due to its breathability.

The natural fabric is also easy to maintain. One sure way to tell good quality cotton is to ensure the material's surface feels smooth, not starched. In addition, you can accessorize your blouse to enable it to stand out in a crowd.

3. Lace

Lace is your best bet if you are looking for a fashionable and feminine fabric. The fabric is unique because it will require a lining to create a beautiful and simple blouse. Lace is made of natural materials and can be made into a wide range of designs, and is occasion-friendly.

Lace can also be a border or an overlay on blouses made from other fabrics. The material has a nice texture and is breathable. However, one of the significant disadvantages is that it can be easily damaged and is pretty expensive.

4. Satin

Satin has a luxurious feel to it and is ideal for making fashionable blouses. You can wear satin to social events like weddings. Satin can prove challenging to sew because of its loop-sided seams, but a good tailor will work through the challenges and produce an elegant garment.

One of the reasons why people pick this fabric over others is because it is wrinkle-resistant, especially the heavier satins. If you select this fabric, it is advisable to follow the washing instructions carefully to ensure the material serves you better. You can also accessorize the smooth, shiny fabric using pearls to add to its beauty.

5. Polyester

Polyester is a synthetic fabric used to make a creative blouse for any occasion. The material has an excellent drape, easily stretches, is stain resistant and is easy to maintain. Polyester is also a strong fibre that is highly resistant to mildew and moth.

The synthetic fabric is unique because it makes and retains excellent pleats. It is also stain and wrinkle-resistant and fades less than cotton. One characteristic of good quality polyester is that it does not develop cut threads after a few washes. This element helps because the material needs to withstand lots of washing.

Unfortunately, this material is prone to runs and pulls. Moreover, it tends to stick to wet skin, which becomes a challenge when you sweat.

6. Knit

Knit is another favourite for many due to its stretch, comfortable fit, and drape. One way to ascertain high-quality knit is to look at the fabric's gauge, which you can do by counting the stitches across vertical rows. A higher gauge means better quality.

This fabric is also unique because it requires a special knit needle, which produces excellent results. Knit also comes in different types, which depend on your blouse design of choice. Therefore, this fabric offers flexibility in terms of the blouse design, which adds variety to your wardrobe.

7. Wool

Gone are the days when wool was only known for making blankets and heavy jackets or sweaters. Today, there is a more refined and softer type of wool that creates beautiful and fashionable blouses.

The soft and elegant fabric is breathable, which makes it ideal for all weather conditions. Wool is natural and highly absorbent and has a soft feel next to the skin. However, the wool fabric does not come cheap and requires specific dry cleaning conditions to maintain its quality.

8. Rayon

Rayon is a light and loose-hanging fabric ideal for making stylish and colourful blouses because it is made mainly from bright fibres. It is a semi-synthetic fabric made from purified wood pulp cellulose fibres. It combines these natural materials with certain chemicals.

The material blends several other fabrics like cotton and polyester, requiring that you follow specific washing instructions. Designers recommend that rayon users hand-wash the material because a washing machine can cause the fabric to stretch or shrink.

9. Linen

Linen fabric has been used for ages and is also known as the richer cousin of cotton material. It contains all the positive cotton qualities and other elegant aspects of its own, making it slightly higher in price than cotton.

Linen is also known as the strongest natural fibre. In addition, it is known for its easy-to-wrinkle quality. The linen material is also a little crispy, so it does not drape on the body. The fabric is also breathable and keeps away moisture.

10. Blends

Blended fabrics are a fusion of fabric fibres that produce a material greater in quality than the original. The blended fabrics have gained popularity and have been used to develop creative and elegant blouse designs.

Some standard blends include linen and cotton, cotton and polyester, rayon and linen, and viscose and polyester. Each fibre brings a different element to the design, including comfort, elasticity, absorbency and bulk. Therefore, blends are affordable, durable and easy to wash.
