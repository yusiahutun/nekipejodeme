35 Bridgerton Terms to Know Ahead of Season 2

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, January 29, 2024

Throw on your most embellished frock and exquisite jewels, a new courting season has arrived! The second season of Bridgerton just dropped on Netflix with Anthony Bridgerton as our main course. Will the former rake live up to the lifestyle of a viscount and find true love? Not without drama at the very least; the season centers on a love triangle between Anthony and two sisters new to the ton: Edwina and Kate Sharma.

Now, of course Lady Whistledown's Society Paper will help you keep up with every twist and turn, but you may want to refresh your Regency-era English so you don’t miss a beat. To help, we've rounded up a list of the most important terms for you to brush up on as you reacquaint yourself with the Upper Orders.

Rake: a historical version of the modern-day f*** boy; a man who isn't interested in settling down, has many romantic encounters, and enjoys a good party

Duke: in high society, this is the highest ranking title after the king

Viscount: right beneath the title of duke, a viscount is a family-earned title in the nobility world that ranks above a baron; typically associated with some type of legal duties

Diamond of the First Water: the season's most stunning debutante, who has beauty, grace, wit, and charm—and can even catch the Queen's eye

The Dark Walk: this refers to a specific area in the Pleasure Gardens where women run the risk of being ruined should they engage in a little after-hours play with a rake; also a dangerous ground for potential assault

Upper Orders: a member of high society or the upper echelon of the ton

Frock: a dress

Lady on Display: a woman engaging in the courting season, showing up dressed to the nines

Spinster: an unmarried woman, generally older of age

Dubious Parentage: someone who comes from a family of questionable origins

High in the Instep: a person of privilege who is aware of their status, makes it known, and who is often snobby or arrogant

Drawing Room: a room in which a host or hostess can entertain their guests

Simpleton: a foolish or stupid person

Coming Out: making one's debut at a ball during the courting season

The Season: the courting season is full of lavish balls for debutants to make their debut as they search for a husband

Being Ruined: having one's reputation slandered; someone who engages in or is part of a scandal

The Marital Act: having sex

Marquees: another member of high society ranking below a duke and above an earl

An Eccentric: an odd or strange person

Promenade: to walk around publicly alongside someone you're courting so everyone knows who you are seeing

Facer: an intense hit to the face, a punch

Fortnight: a period of two weeks time

Cucumberish: to be without funds or money

Ton: high society members belonging to a town or city, who generally have their fair share of power and money

Pleasure Gardens: a scenic park that is open to the public for recreational use

Courses: a woman's menstrual cycle

Society Papers: the Ton’s gossip column

Corinthian: a well-respected athlete who is competent in most sporting events (boxing, hunting, fencing, etc.)

Sire an Heir: the act of having a child who will inherit the family's name and assets

Blue-Deviled: depressed, withdrawn, melancholy

Swoon: to faint dramatically and, usually, for attention

Snuff: ground up tobacco that someone inhales for a quick boost of energy

Modiste: the Ton's most sought-after dressmaker

River Tick: a person who is in debt or has little to no money

Countenance: someone's facial expression
