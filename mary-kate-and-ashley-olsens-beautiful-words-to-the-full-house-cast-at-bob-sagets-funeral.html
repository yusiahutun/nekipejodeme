Mary Kate and Ashley Olsen's 'beautiful' words to the Full House cast at Bob Saget's funeral

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, June 28, 2024

Bob Saget’s funeral in 2022 was attended by the cast of Full House, including Mary Kate and Ashley Olsen. Recently, John Stamos revealed what ‘beautiful’ words were said by the twins.

The hit TV show ran for eight seasons from 1987, with the Olsen twins sharing the role of Michelle Tanner. They were joined by Bob Saget, who played the role of family patriarch, Danny Tanner.

Mary Kate and Ashley Olsen’s ‘beautiful’ words at Bob Saget’s funeral

The two did not speak at the actor’s funeral, however, John Stamos recently revealed that the two did attend, and said it was “so beautiful” during an interview on  SiriusXM’s, The Howard Stern Show.

“They got us all together and said, ‘We love you. We loved our childhood. We’re grateful for you guys, we thank you for making those eight years so beautiful for us. We have such fond memories.’”

“Everybody just needed to hear that,” the 60-year-old added.

The hit show went on to land a sequel, Fuller House, which aired on Netflix, however, the twins did not take part.

Speaking on why they didn’t join, Stamos revealed he was “a little upset at first” as it’d be ‘nice to have them on there.’

However, he said, “They just didn’t want to do it, they weren’t acting at the time and I don’t think a real offer came through.”

The actor also speaks about his relationship with the twins in his new memoir, as he writes: “I adore them, and they adore me.”

Bob Saget was found dead in January 2022

On January 9 2022, Saget was found dead in his Orlando hotel room. After investigations from the authorities, his family revealed:

“They have concluded that he accidentally hit the back of his head on something, thought nothing of it, and went to sleep. No drugs or alcohol were involved.”

The Sheriff’s Office also said there were no signs of ‘foul play.’

At the time of his tragic passing, many celebrity tributes came flooding in for the beloved star, including John Stamos, who shared an Instagram tribute to his beloved friend and co-star, on behalf of the cast.

In part of the statement, he penned: “Thirty-five years ago, we came together as a TV family, but we became a real family. And now we grieve as a family. Bob made us laugh until we cried.

“Now our tears flow in sadness, but also with gratitude for all the beautiful memories of our sweet, kind, hilarious, cherished Bob. He was a brother to us guys, a father to us girls, and a friend to all of us. Bob, we love you dearly.”
