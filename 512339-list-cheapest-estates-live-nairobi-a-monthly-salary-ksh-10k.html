List of Cheapest Estates to Live in Nairobi Metropolitan with Monthly Salary of KSh 10k

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, July 20, 2024
  • Kenya's capital Nairobi has an estimated population of 5.3 million, according to the World Population Review
  • The city's population is expected to double to 8.4 million by 2035, meaning the demand for housing would increase
  • Some areas such as Kangemi, Ruai and Wangige within the Nairobi Metropolitan area have affordable houses for low-income earners

Kenya's capital Nairobi has an estimated population of 5.3 million, according to the World Population Review.

The independent organisation noted the city's population increased by over 205,000 people in the last year.

Nairobi's population is expected to double to 8.4 million by 2035, meaning the demand for housing would increase. Habitat for Humanity revealed that Kenya has an annual housing demand of 250,000 units.

However, there is an estimated supply of 50,000 units, culminating in a housing deficit of 2 million units.

Whereas Nairobi has some of the most expensive estates in Africa, some areas within the Nairobi Metropolitan area have affordable houses for low-income earners.

With housing being a basic need besides food and clothing, the United Nations (UN) recommends that monthly rent should not exceed 30% of a household’s income.

A report by Cytonn Investments Ltd, which deals in real estate, showed some of the lower middle areas to live within the Nairobi Metropolitan area.

"For the lowest rents in the market without compromising on the quality of the house, one can always look to areas such as Rongai, Kiserian, Matasia, Ngong, Kasarani and Kinoo," the report stated.

Who Owns Kenya reported that Kenyans earning less than KSh 10,000 who cannot afford to live in permanent houses resort to living in mabati houses.

Cheapest estates to live in Nairobi Metropolitan

1. Ruiru

Ruiru in Kiambu county is a 40-minute drive from the central business district.

The town has a population of over 490,000 people, most of whom work in Nairobi.

Single rooms in the area cost an average of KSh 3,000 monthly while those wishing to live in bedsitters will cough up over KSh 5,000.

2. Kangemi/Uthiru

Uthiru and Kangemi located on Waiyaki Way are home to middle and lower class citizens.

Single mabati houses in the area cost roughly KSh 4,000. On the other hand, bedsitters in the areas cost between KSh 6,000 and KSh 10,000.

3. Ruai and Gachie

Renting a single room mabati house in Gachie and Ruai costs between KSh 2,000 and KSh 3,000.

Block single rooms, meanwhile, cost between KSh 3,000 and KSh 4,000.

Single self-bedsitters, on the other hand, cost an estimated KSh 5,500 monthly in rent.

4. Kinoo

Kinoo is located in Kiambu county on the way to Kikuyu town.

The cheapest mabati house in the area goes for KSh 1,500 while the rent for block single rooms ranges from KSh 3,500 to KSh 4,000.

If you want to live in a bedsitter, the cheapest starts from KSh 6,000.

5. Wangige

Located in Kabete constituency along the Western Bypass Highway, Wangige offers some of the most affordable houses for low-income earners.

The cost of the cheapest houses in the area range from KSh 1,500 to KSh 6,000.

Most expensive neighbourhoods in Nairobi

Gigiri suburb is home to the most expensive houses in Nairobi.

Gigiri is home to Kenya's large expatriate community. It hosts one of the largest ex-pat communities in Africa.

The high-end residential area hosts the United Nations Office, the United Nations Environmental Programme (UNEP), the United Nations Settlement Programme, and the United States Embassy.
