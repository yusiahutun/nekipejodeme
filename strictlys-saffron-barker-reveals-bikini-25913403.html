Strictlys Saffron Barker reveals bikini line tattoo request that left men baffled

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, July 31, 2024

Former Strictly Come Dancing star Saffron Barker has opened up about her tiny tattoos, including the three words she had imprinted on her bikini line.

Saffron rose to fame sharing videos with a growing subscriber base on YouTube, before she appeared on Strictly in 2019.

The vlogger was partnered with AJ Pritchard, and the duo were eventually eliminated in the tenth live show.

More recently, Saffron featured on a celebrity series of The Circle alongside the heavily tattooed Pete Wicks, and shortly after she spoke to the TOWIE star about her ink on his ‘Just a Little Prick’ podcast.

“You probably wouldn’t even class them as tattoos,” she began. “I’ve got four, I would actually say three and a half, maybe three and a quarter, because one just really doesn’t count.”

The star’s first tattoo is of a tiny red heart on her pinky finger, and Saffron noted: “Every tattoo I’ve got, I just decided on the day that I wanted to get.”

And, the YouTuber explained that the decision to get her most recent tattoo had followed the same process, remembering: “I walked in there and I thought, ‘what is empowering that I can have on my bikini line?’

“I walked in, and get ready for this, and I said what I wanted to get done at the top of my head, and all the men went, ‘huh?’, and all the women went, ‘yes girl!’

“So I walked in and I said, ‘I would like, on my bikini line, ‘she is art’. It just came to me, like that.

“I think I was having a lot of self-love that day… like anyone I’ve always had body insecurity so where this came from, I’ve got no idea.”

Continuing to run through her ink, Saffron said that another tattoo – of the word ‘always’ on her arm – is a matching inscription that she got with her mum.

“This was very spontaneous. My mum has never wanted a tattoo ever in her life, and I have this weird thing of convincing her to do things, so one day I was like, ‘shall we just go and get a tattoo?’”

Trying to describe the reasoning behind the tattoo, Saffron explained: “When we argue, we’ll always say ‘always’, as in ‘always together’.”

“I have four tattoos but I do actually want so many more. But my problem is, I only like quotes. I don’t like a picture enough for it to be on my body… But I’m going to end up looking like a walking dictionary.

As an example, Saffron admitted that another tattoo she decided to make permanent on her body reads, ‘Dare to Dream’.

“It’s just there I guess to remind myself that I should always dream and not let people tell me otherwise,” she added.
