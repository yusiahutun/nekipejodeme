Man Utd laughing and Liverpool loving: Every Premier League team's fan's most-used emoji

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, July 8, 2024

The most used emoji’s for the fans of each Premier League club have been revealed - and it’s fair to say they tell the story of their season!

A study from William Hill has gone into some serious depth about how fans write about their clubs on Twitter, exploring nearly 30 million tweets from over 920,000 supporters.

The results seem to reflect their seasons too, in all fairness, with Liverpool’s most used emoji unsurprisingly the red dot symbol, while the raised hands in celebrations comes second, depicting someone saying ‘give me ten’, and the red love heart in third.

Meanwhile, it’s a slightly sadder state of affairs at Old Trafford, with most fans using the crying with laughter emoji about Manchester United, who made their worst start to a season this term.

Many clubs have an emoji resembling their nickname or mascot as their most used symbol - Crystal Palace have an eagle, while Wolves have a wolf and Leicester supporters often use a fox.

A dot resembling club colours are the most used for a lot of sides, including Tottenham and Arsenal, while Chelsea fans are giving a little more love with a blue heart, as are Norwich fans, whose tally is decked out with yellow and green coloured symbols (no raised hands there, that’s for sure!).

West Ham actually have the highest use of one emoji; sadly there’ll be no prizes for the right guess though - Hammers followers always seem to be using the Hammer and Pick emoji, which is almost a carbon copy of their club crest.

The football emoji is used for quite a few teams, as is the laughing face, and if you’re a Southampton fan, the angel emoji, presumably representing St Mary’s, their stadium.

“Perhaps the most interesting of all is how Manchester United fans react to on and off the field action, and the laughing face emoji is used more than any other.
