Best Outdoor Restaurants in NYC for 2014 via The Infatuation

1) Vinegar Hill House

Media Platforms Design Team

You really can't beat an evening in the garden at Vinegar Hill House. This is one of the best outdoor dining situations in all of NYC, thanks to the fact that it's sickeningly cute back there, and the food happens to be excellent too.

72 Hudson Avenue, Brooklyn, vinegarhillhouse.com.

2

2) Narcissa

Media Platforms Design Team

This new restaurant at the Standard Hotel East Village is one of our favorite new places for 2014, and we like it even more now that they opened up their outdoor dining space, which is pretty much unbeatable for brunch. Also, carrot fries.

21 Cooper Square, narcissarestaurant.com.

3) Montmartre

Media Platforms Design Team

With the chef from Perla in the kitchen, the food at Montmarte is on point. The back patio is also on point, and there's even a separate bar out there from which you can drink a whole bunch of ros√© on a Friday afternoon. Get the arctic char salad.

158 8th Avenue, montmartenyc.com.

4

4) Lavender Lake

Media Platforms Design Team

Situated in Brooklyn along the picturesque Gowanus Canal, a visit to Lavender Lake is like taking a trip to the French Riviera. Just kidding. It's like going to a chill outdoor bar with really good food. And when it comes to New York City in the summer, sometimes that's all you need.

383 Carroll Street, Brooklyn, lavendarlake.com.

5

5) Barbuto

Media Platforms Design Team

So this isn't technically outdoor dining, but Barbuto has huge garage doors that open to the street so that you can catch a breeze while you get down on their legendary roasted chicken dish. You'll also see lots of fashion-types in here, thanks to the fact that it's next door to the Industria photo studio. Better bring your shoe game.

775 Washington Street, barbutonyc.com.

6

6) Pure Food & Wine

Media Platforms Design Team

Pure Food And Wine has a special place in our hearts, not only because eating here occasionally has probably saved our lives (thanks nutrients), but also because the garden behind the restaurant is huge and ridiculously nice. Raw food has never tasted so good.

54 Irving Plaza, oneluckyduck.com.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

7) Maison Premiere

Media Platforms Design Team

Oysters, absinthe, and a back yard that looks like it belongs in the Garden District of New Orleans. Head to Williamsburg and hit Maison Premiere one day this summer. You'll see why it's always at the top of lists like this.

298 Bedford Avenue, Brooklyn, maisonpremiere.com.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

8

8) Frankies457

Media Platforms Design Team

This place is an O.G. when it comes to the Brooklyn restaurant scene. Frankies has been around since way before Brooklyn got all Urban Outfitters, and their outdoor dining setup is off the hook. Not only is there a back yard, but they also have a "barn" that you can rent out for parties, weddings, get togethers...really anything you want to celebrate by doing something awesome.

457 Court Street, Brooklyn, frankiessputino.com.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

9

9) Gallow Green at The McKittrick Hotel

Media Platforms Design Team

Gallow Green is the bar and restaurant that sits atop the McKittrick Hotel, home to Sleep No More. It's a beautifully appointed space, and even though it's open to the public (no ticket required), it's definitely still part of the show. That means something weird is probably gonna happen to you while you have your drinks and dinner. Good weird.

542 West 27th Street, mckittrickhotel.com.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

10) East Pole

Media Platforms Design Team

A new restaurant on the Upper East Side, brought to you by the team behind the Fat Radish. That means East Pole is way cooler and far better than pretty much everything else in the east 60's, and they also have a little patio below the sidewalk that's perfect for daytime hanging. Hit this place before or after a visit to Central Park. It's your best option for a mile.

133 East 65th Street, theeastpolenyc.com.
