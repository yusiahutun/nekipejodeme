14 of the Best Cat Trees for Your Feline Friends

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, February 25, 2024

We’ve all seen ’em — those unsightly cat trees you’d rather place next to the dumpster than front and center in your home for all the world to see. And yet — you know how much joy a kitty palace, what with all its little nooks, crannies, and toys, would bring to your pet’s life. You don’t want to deprive your beloved animal, but you also don’t want an eyesore sitting smack dab in the middle of the pristine living room you’ve worked so hard to make inviting. What’s a gal to do? 

Think outside the box, of course! Thanks to advancements in pet design, the best cat trees on the market that won’t make you cringe upon sight. In fact, some are so sleek, they merely look like attractive furnishings you purchased to enhance the style of your room! 

Offering up the same cozy resting places and scratching pads your cat has come to expect from a traditional cat tree in newly innovate, aesthetically pleasing designs, you’ll be chomping at the bit to introduce these pet condos to your home — and your feline friend will thank you for it.

How to Choose the Best Cat Tree for Your Pet

In addition to a stylish or elegant aesthetic, there are a number of factors you should consider when deciding which type of tree your fluffy sidekick might like best. 

Firstly, you’ll want to take his or her weight into consideration. If you’re selecting a product that’s only built for a 15-pound cat, and your pet weighs 20+ pounds (yes, it’s possible!), it’s likely not a match. You don’t want your precious baby getting hurt on a tree that’s not built to accommodate its size! 

You’ll also want to take into account the size of your space. While most trees range from 28 to 72 inches, a larger model may not be a fit for that tiny corner you had in mind. Likewise, a miniature version might seem a bit out of place in a large room with plenty of floor space to fill. 

Finally, your cat’s personal preferences can also play a role in helping you find the purr-fect design. Some may prefer carpet to wood, for instance, while others rest easier in hammocks than caves. You know your kitty best, so shop accordingly!
